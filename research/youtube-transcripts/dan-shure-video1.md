Title: AI Changed SEO: What Still Wins in SEO in 2025

Author: Dan Shure

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9gH1k5BO7bs

## Overview

This conversation features Dan Shure discussing the impact of AI on SEO, content quality, and the shift away from user-focused content toward mass AI-generated output.

Core themes:
- AI-generated content is largely ineffective for SEO performance
- Content is being commoditized due to AI tools
- SEO has shifted away from user-centric thinking
- Real value comes from unique insights, data, and human expertise
- AI should be used as an assistive tool, not a replacement for thinking


## Workflow / Process

### 1. Content Creation in the AI Era

Traditional approach:
- Research topic
- Create content brief
- Write original content
- Optimize and publish

Current issue:
- Writers using AI to generate full content
- Lack of originality and value
- Poor ranking performance

Recommended approach:
- Use AI only as a support tool
- Maintain human-led content creation
- Focus on adding unique value

---

### 2. Recommended AI Usage (3 Practical Methods)

#### Method 1: Deep Research

Use AI to:
- Gather structured data
- Compile lists (e.g., conferences, tools, products)
- Extract relevant attributes

Process:
1. Provide clear query and requirements
2. Let AI collect data
3. Manually assemble and refine content

---

#### Method 2: Repurposing Original Content

Use AI to:
- Process transcripts (calls, podcasts, interviews)
- Summarize and restructure content

Key principle:
- Input must be original (your thoughts, expertise)

Examples:
- Client calls → blog content
- Podcasts → articles
- PDFs → summarized insights

---

#### Method 3: “What NOT to Do” Outlining

Process:
1. Ask AI to generate a standard outline
2. Use it as a reference for:
   - Common patterns
   - Overused structures
3. Deliberately differentiate from it

Goal:
- Avoid generic, repetitive content structures

---

### 3. Content Brief Strategy

Key components:

1. Competitive examples:
   - Identify low domain authority pages ranking well
   - Use as benchmarks

2. Custom structure:
   - Tailored outline per topic
   - Not formulaic

3. Audience + intent guidance:
   - Define target user
   - Define intent:
     - Informational vs transactional
     - Passive vs actionable

Key insight:
- Content briefs cannot be fully automated due to nuance

---

### 4. Quality Control Challenge (AI Content Detection)

Problem:
- Writers secretly using AI
- AI detection tools are unreliable

Observed pattern:
- AI-generated content:
  - Fails to rank
  - May not even get indexed
  - Underperforms even in low-competition scenarios

Conclusion:
- Requires manual review + trust with writers
- No reliable automated detection solution

---

### 5. User-Centric Content Strategy

Core process:

1. Identify target audience
2. Understand:
   - Problems
   - Emotions
   - Context of search

3. Create content that:
   - Solves real problems
   - Reflects actual user needs

4. Study real behavior:
   - Surveys
   - Interviews
   - Direct feedback

Key shift:
- From “ranking content” → “helping people”

---

### 6. Differentiation Strategy

To stand out:

- Add:
  - Unique data
  - Unique opinions
  - First-hand insights

- Avoid:
  - Generic summaries
  - Rewritten content

Example:
- Instead of listing standard SaaS metrics:
  - Challenge outdated metrics
  - Provide new frameworks

---

### 7. Platform Diversification

Observation:
- Platforms like TikTok function as search engines

Strategy:
- Publish across platforms
- Leverage long-tail visibility from social content

Example:
- TikTok videos gaining traffic for over a year

---

### 8. AI Workflow Integration (Limited Use)

Effective use case:

- Meeting tools (e.g., Fathom):
  - Transcription
  - Summarization
  - Action items extraction

Use cases:
- Query meeting content
- Extract decisions and tasks

Observation:
- One of the few AI uses that significantly improves productivity

## Key Insights

### 1. AI-generated content does not perform well
- Often fails to rank
- Lacks originality
- Provides no new value

---

### 2. Content is being commoditized
- AI enables mass production
- Reduces perceived value of content

---

### 3. SEO has lost focus on users
- Shift toward:
  - Speed
  - Scale
  - Automation

- Away from:
  - Helping people
  - Solving real problems

---

### 4. Originality is the main differentiator
- Unique thoughts > reworded content
- First-hand insights > aggregated summaries

---

### 5. AI should support, not replace thinking
Best uses:
- Research
- Organization
- Repurposing

Worst use:
- Full content generation

---

### 6. AI content creates a feedback loop problem
- LLMs trained on AI-generated content
- Leads to:
  - Lower quality outputs
  - Recycled information

---

### 7. Creativity is a competitive advantage
- Most websites look and sound the same
- Opportunity to:
  - Be different
  - Experiment with format and design

---

### 8. SEO is not dead
- Claims of “SEO is dead” are often:
  - Marketing-driven
  - Used to sell new services

---

### 9. AI is overhyped
- LLMs = advanced text prediction
- Not true intelligence

---

### 10. Human expertise remains critical
- Especially for:
  - Strategy
  - Content direction
  - Insight generation


## Results / Outcomes

Observed outcomes:

- AI-generated content:
  - Underperforms in rankings
  - May not index
  - Fails in expected easy opportunities

- Human-created content:
  - Continues to perform better
  - Provides competitive advantage

- TikTok / social content:
  - Can generate long-term visibility
  - Acts as a search engine


## Limitations / Notes

### AI Limitations

- No original thinking
- Produces generic outputs
- Detection tools unreliable

---

### Operational Challenges

- Difficult to enforce content quality across teams
- Writers may hide AI usage
- Requires ongoing education of clients

---

### Industry Challenges

- Overhype around AI
- Misaligned incentives (clickbait, selling services)
- Resource misallocation by companies

---

### Strategic Risk

- Over-reliance on AI leads to:
  - Low-quality content
  - Poor performance
  - Loss of differentiation

---

### Future Direction

- Emphasis on:
  - Authenticity
  - Human insight
  - Creativity
  - User-first content
