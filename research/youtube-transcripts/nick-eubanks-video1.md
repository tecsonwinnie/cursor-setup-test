Title: Nick Eubanks (Semrush VP) on AI Visibility, Evidence Clusters & the Future of Search

Author: Nick Eubanks

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QscD4VCWUoY

## Overview

This conversation features Nick Eubanks discussing AI-driven SEO, content experiments with fully AI-generated content, and how visibility in LLMs differs from traditional Google SEO.

Core themes:
- AI visibility requires different tactics than traditional SEO
- “Search everywhere optimization” replaces siloed SEO
- LLMs prioritize semantic relevance, freshness, and sentiment
- AI content can rank in LLMs without authority or backlinks
- UGC platforms (Reddit, Quora, LinkedIn) are critical for visibility
- Evidence-based content improves trust and citations


## Workflow / Process

### 1. AI Visibility Strategy (Search Everywhere Optimization)

Step 1: Expand beyond traditional SEO
- Treat all platforms as search engines:
  - Google
  - ChatGPT
  - LinkedIn
  - Reddit
  - TikTok / Instagram

Step 2: Build multi-surface visibility
- Ensure presence across:
  - Website
  - UGC platforms
  - Social channels

Step 3: Align content production + distribution
- Start with core content (blog)
- Break into smaller pieces
- Distribute across platforms

---

### 2. Content Production Pipeline (AI Era)

Beginning of lifecycle:
- Identify what matters across platforms (not just keywords)
- Build content briefs based on:
  - Multi-platform demand
  - Intent + semantic relevance

End of lifecycle:
- Distribute content across:
  - Twitter/X
  - LinkedIn
  - Reddit
  - Quora

Key shift:
- Content lifecycle now includes **distribution + platform adaptation**

---

### 3. AI Content Experiment (Zero Authority Site)

Setup:
- New WordPress site
- No backlinks
- No authority
- No trust signals
- ~20 AI-generated posts per week
- No editing or human review

Observed behavior:
- No Google visibility
- Indexed but extremely low authority
- Appears in ChatGPT responses
- Gets cited directly in LLM outputs

Traffic:
- Users copy/paste ChatGPT outputs mentioning the site
- Drives real users and newsletter signups

Conclusion:
- LLM visibility does NOT require:
  - Backlinks
  - Domain authority
  - Google rankings

### 5. Reddit Strategy (AI Visibility)

Layer 1: Brand presence
- Monitor mentions
- Respond helpfully
- Address negative sentiment

Layer 2: Non-brand strategy
- Use real accounts (not fake/spam)
- Avoid promotion
- Ask thoughtful questions

Key tactic:
- Ask questions → trigger community discussion → influence perception

Goal:
- Drive brand-modified searches
- Encourage organic advocacy

Constraints:
- Reddit is difficult:
  - Shadowbans
  - Account trust requirements
  - Anti-spam detection

---

### 6. Semrush AI Share of Voice Framework

Step 1: Define “concepts” (not keywords)
- Extract intent behind prompts
- Group similar prompts into concepts

Step 2: Analyze prompts at scale
- Example: 500 prompts relevant to audience

Step 3: Measure:
- Brand mentions
- Position in responses
- Sentiment (positive/negative)

Step 4: Optimize content

Key tactic:
- Inject missing semantic relevance into existing content:
  - Add sentences (not just links)
  - Expand topical coverage

---

### 7. Content Optimization for LLMs

Tactics:

- Match heading → first sentence
- Answer questions immediately
- Use clear, direct language
- Avoid:
  - Analogies
  - Metaphors
  - Unclear phrasing

- Use:
  - Verifiable claims
  - Up-to-date facts
  - Structured answers

---

### 8. Evidence Cluster Strategy

Definition:
- Instead of keyword clusters → build **evidence clusters**

Execution:
- Aggregate multiple sources
- Link extensively to supporting data
- Provide layered validation:
  - Studies
  - Reports
  - Secondary references

Pattern:
- Over-index on citations
- Provide more evidence than necessary

Outcome:
- Increased trust from LLMs
- Higher likelihood of citation

---

### 9. Content Distribution Strategy

Approach:
- Start with one core asset
- Repurpose across platforms

Important nuance:
- Same topic ≠ same format

Adjust for:
- Platform behavior
- Audience expectations

Examples:
- Blog ≠ Reddit thread
- Instagram ≠ YouTube
- Facebook allows long-form video uploads

---

### 10. Team Structure Shift

Old model:

New model:
- Channel-specific ownership

Examples:
- Dedicated YouTube owner
- Dedicated Reddit owner

Implication:
- Teams structured by platform expertise, not brand

## Key Insights

### 1. AI visibility ≠ traditional SEO
- No backlinks required (in some cases)
- No authority required (in some cases)
- Semantic relevance is critical

---

### 2. LLMs reward semantic density
- Large volumes of tightly focused content improve visibility
- Topic depth matters more than authority signals

---

### 3. Sentiment affects visibility
- Negative Reddit sentiment → reduces inclusion
- Positive sentiment → increases inclusion

---

### 4. UGC platforms are critical
- Reddit, Quora, LinkedIn heavily cited
- Influence both AI and traditional search

---

### 5. “Search everywhere” replaces SEO
- All platforms act as search engines
- Visibility must be multi-channel

---

### 6. Evidence > opinions
- More citations → more trust
- Aggregation can outperform original data

---

### 7. AI traffic is polarized
- Some report:
  - High-quality leads
- Others report:
  - Low-quality traffic

---

### 8. Conversion rates from AI can be higher
- Internal study:
  - ~4.4x higher conversion vs Google traffic

---

### 9. Freshness matters more in LLMs
- Preference for recent content
- Faster content cycles required

---

### 10. Short-term vs long-term tactics

Short-term:
- Some “old spam tactics” working again in LLMs

Long-term:
- Likely unsustainable
- Google expected to normalize quality signals

## Limitations / Notes

### AI Content Risks
- Connecting to Google Search Console may trigger penalties
- Pure AI content may be unsafe for long-term SEO

---

### Platform Constraints

Reddit:
- Strict anti-spam systems
- Requires real accounts
- Difficult to scale

---

### AI System Limitations
- LLM sourcing still unclear (e.g., Bing, tweets, unknown sources)
- Attribution pathways not fully transparent

---

### Strategic Trade-offs

- Short-term gains vs long-term sustainability
- Brand risk vs experimentation
- Enterprise constraints (budgets, silos)

---

### Future Outlook

- Google likely to dominate AI search
- AI mode may become default (~Q3 2026 estimate)
- Traditional SEO signals may regain importance
