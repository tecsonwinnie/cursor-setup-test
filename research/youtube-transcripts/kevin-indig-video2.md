Title: Moving Beyond Old SEO Models in the Age of AI

Author: Kevin Indig

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AqAoKGftsSE

Key Topics:
- Shift from keyword-based SEO to entity-driven SEO
- Rise of AI search and declining importance of clicks
- SEO evolving into brand, PR, and multi-channel strategy
- Agentic search and future AI-driven user journeys
- Importance of trust, context, and internal data

## Overview

This conversation features Kevin Indig discussing the evolving landscape of AI-driven search, including competition between Google and ChatGPT, the shift from keyword-based SEO to topic/intent-based optimization, and the future of agentic search.

Core themes:
- AI search is expanding the SEO surface rather than replacing it
- Shift from keywords → topics, intent, and entities
- AI is changing both search behavior and SEO workflows
- Agentic search and automation will reshape e-commerce and technical SEO
- Trust, reputation, and context are becoming central to visibility


## Workflow / Process

### 1. AI Search Strategy Shift

Step 1: Move from keyword-based thinking
- Prompts are longer and more variable
- Keyword aggregation becomes less useful

Step 2: Focus on:
- Topics
- Intent signals
- Entities

Step 3: Build strategy around:
- Topic clusters
- Intent mapping
- Entity associations

---

### 2. AI Analytics & Data Modeling

Problem:
- Prompts are too long and inconsistent for traditional keyword tracking

Proposed solution:
- Aggregate data into:
  - Topics
  - Intent categories

Future analytics likely include:
- Topic trends (similar to Google Trends)
- Intent signals
- Possibly demographic overlays (anonymized)

---

### 3. AI Content & Context Optimization

Key concept: **Context Engineering**

Process:
1. Identify existing internal content:
   - Help docs
   - PDFs
   - Subdomains
   - Internal knowledge bases (e.g., Confluence)

2. Extract usable knowledge (non-sensitive)

3. Convert into:
   - External-facing content
   - Structured knowledge for AI consumption

4. Ensure brand-controlled information is surfaced instead of third-party narratives

---

### 4. Omnichannel + AI Visibility Workflow

- Extend SEO beyond website content into:
  - LinkedIn
  - Reddit
  - Quora
  - Other UGC platforms

- Adapt content style per platform
- Align messaging across channels

Goal:
- Influence AI training and retrieval sources

---

### 5. Agentic Search Preparation (Emerging)

Phase 1 (Preparation):
- Improve technical foundations
- Simplify navigation
- Reduce friction for bots/agents

Phase 2 (Optimization):
- Enable agent-friendly experiences
- Monitor agent interactions

Future metric:
- Agentic completion rate
  - % of agent visits that complete tasks (e.g., checkout)

---

### 6. Technical Optimization for Agents

Critical factors:
- Simple navigation
- Minimal friction elements:
  - Popups
  - Cookie banners
  - Heavy JavaScript

Important areas:
- Semantic HTML
- CSS simplicity
- Server response time (critical)
- Core Web Vitals (more important for agents)

Future concept:
- Agent-specific schema
- Defined interaction paths for AI agents

---

### 7. Workflow Automation (Internal SEO Operations)

AI enables:
- Automation of repetitive tasks
- Faster data collection (e.g., SERP scraping)
- Advanced clustering and analysis

Example workflow:
1. Collect SERP data (e.g., via APIs)
2. Analyze SERP features (video, images, etc.)
3. Build detailed clusters:
   - Topic clusters
   - Journey-based clusters
   - Persona-based clusters
4. Feed into AI systems
5. Generate drafts or briefs
6. Human refinement

## Key Insights

### 1. Google vs ChatGPT is not zero-sum
- Users use both platforms
- SEO surface area is expanding, not shrinking

---

### 2. Google has structural advantage
- Hardware
- Data
- Distribution
- Ecosystem integration

ChatGPT advantage:
- Momentum
- Cultural adoption
- Subscription willingness

---

### 3. Keywords are becoming obsolete as primary unit
- Prompts are too long and variable
- Topics and intent are more stable units

---

### 4. Entity-based search is critical
- Google recognizes brands, products, and relationships
- Entity recognition extends beyond exact keywords

---

### 5. SEO is both expanding and fragmenting
- More vertical specialization (local, AI, etc.)
- More cross-functional work (PR, brand, social)

---

### 6. Trust is central to AI search
- Users must trust sources and systems
- AI adoption depends on trust (especially for transactions)

---

### 7. Reputation management is critical in LLMs
- LLMs can surface:
  - Third-party opinions
  - Incorrect or manipulated data

Mitigation:
- Publish authoritative internal content
- Control brand narrative

---

### 8. AI systems will evolve to validate information
- Concepts:
  - Critical agents
  - Agent debate systems
  - Reliability scoring layers

Purpose:
- Filter low-quality or manipulated content

---

### 9. Agentic search is coming but not immediate
- Current agents are unstable
- Adoption will take time
- Likely gradual rollout over years

---

### 10. Visual and multimodal search is growing
- Google Lens usage is significant
- Use cases:
  - Product discovery
  - Translation
  - Local/business lookup

---

### 11. AI shifts SEO from clicks → influence
- Traffic may decline
- Influence across channels increases importance

---

### 12. AI transforms SEO roles

Shift from:
- Recommendation-based role

To:
- Execution + production role:
  - Content generation
  - Prototyping
  - Workflow building

---

### 13. Human role evolves, not disappears
- Humans become:
  - Editors
  - Directors
  - Supervisors

AI increases:
- Output
- Speed
- Capability

---

## Results / Outcomes

Observed trends:

- Industries already negatively impacted by AI:
  - Translation services
  - Stack Overflow
  - Chegg

- Increased adoption of:
  - Workflow automation in marketing teams

- SEO shifting toward:
  - Influence-based outcomes instead of traffic-based

## Limitations / Notes

### AI Search Limitations

- Prompt variability makes tracking difficult
- Lack of standardized analytics
- Personalization adds complexity

---

### Agentic Search Constraints

- Agents currently:
  - Unstable
  - Prone to failure

Technical blockers:
- Popups
- JS-heavy interfaces
- Slow performance

---

### Adoption Constraints

- Trust is the main barrier
- Users may resist delegating decisions (e.g., payments)
- Full adoption may take years (possibly a decade)

---

### Strategic Guidance

- Do not abandon classic SEO
- Continue investing in:
  - Technical SEO
  - Content quality
  - Site architecture
  - Internal linking

- Prepare for future systems without overreacting
