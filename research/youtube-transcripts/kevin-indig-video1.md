Title: SEO in the Age of AI | Kevin Indig on Google Overviews, E-Commerce & The Future of Search

Author: Kevin Indig

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qujABKOAThA

## Overview

The conversation features Kevin Indig discussing how AI overviews and LLM-driven search are reshaping SEO.

Core theme:
- Search is shifting from click-driven → answer-driven
- Visibility no longer guarantees traffic
- SEO must adapt to AI-first discovery, fragmented funnels, and new KPIs

The discussion covers:
- Changes in SERP features (AI overviews, product grids, images)
- LLM citation behavior (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google)
- Content structure and optimization for AI
- New measurement frameworks for SEO performance
- Strategic decisions like blocking AI crawlers


## Workflow / Process

### 1. SERP & AI Overview Analysis

- Analyze large query datasets (e.g., 35,000 queries via SEOClarity)
- Track presence of:
  - AI overviews
  - Product grids
  - Image carousels
  - Forums/discussions
- Compare year-over-year changes in feature dominance


### 2. AI / LLM Optimization Workflow

Step 1: Identify dominant platforms
- Prioritize based on impact:
  - ChatGPT (largest returns)
  - Gemini (fastest growth)
  - Others: Perplexity, Copilot, Claude

Step 2: Understand citation sources per platform
- ChatGPT → Wikipedia-heavy
- Perplexity & Google → Reddit-heavy
- Others → mixed sources

Step 3: Optimize presence across ecosystems
- Wikipedia → ensure accurate brand representation
- Reddit → participate or influence discussions
- YouTube → create videos targeting specific prompts
- LinkedIn → publish newsletters / articles
- Niche sites → industry-specific authority platforms (e.g., G2, Healthline)


### 3. Content Optimization for AI & SEO

Structure-first approach:
- Use clear heading hierarchy (semantic, not just visual)
- Write in active voice
- Simplify sentence structure (readability / Flesch score)

Add “scannable” elements:
- Table of contents
- FAQs
- Key takeaways
- Summaries

Content pattern:
- Start with direct answer (top of page)
- Then expand into detail (pyramid structure)


### 4. BFCM (Black Friday / Cyber Monday) Strategy

Timeline approach:
- Start promotions early (October)
- Gradually roll out discounts by:
  - Product
  - Category
  - Weekly campaigns

Content & SEO:
- Create new landing pages each year
- Reuse backlinks from previous years
- Monitor AI overview presence for BFCM queries

SERP optimization:
- Enhance product images with promo labels
- Focus on visual presentation (image carousels increasing)


### 5. Decision Framework: Block vs Allow AI Crawlers

Step 1: Cost-benefit analysis
- Current traffic from:
  - Google
  - AI chatbots
- Content production cost
- Risk over 3–5 years

Step 2: Forecast future value
- AI chatbot traffic growing rapidly (~700% YoY cited)
- Consider long-term upside

Step 3: Strategic choice
- Options:
  - Allow crawling
  - Block bots (robots.txt / Cloudflare)
  - Adopt “pay-per-crawl” models


### 6. SEO Measurement Shift

Old funnel:
- Search → Click → Convert

New funnel:
- Search → Read → Influence → Later action

New measurement approach:
- Leading indicators:
  - Rankings
  - Visibility
  - Share of voice
- Supporting signals:
  - Brand recall
  - Trust
  - Mentions
- Lagging:
  - Revenue / conversions (still anchor metric)

## Key Insights

### 1. Clicks are no longer the primary goal
- AI reduces exploratory clicks
- Remaining clicks are:
  - Intentional
  - Action-driven


### 2. AI overviews are replacing traditional SERP elements
- Product grids declining
- Image carousels increasing
- AI becoming dominant shopping interface


### 3. Trust precedes relevance

New user behavior:
1. “Do I trust this source?”
2. “Is it relevant?”

Implication:
- Brand familiarity is critical before search


### 4. LLM optimization is platform-specific
- Different models use different sources
- Requires multi-platform presence strategy


### 5. Content simplicity is a competitive advantage
- Not new, but amplified by AI
- Structure + clarity improves:
  - User comprehension
  - AI parsing


### 6. Forums decreasing in SERP ≠ decreasing influence
- Reddit visibility in SERP packs decreasing
- Influence in AI answers increasing


### 7. Query fan-out increases importance of topic coverage
- AI expands queries into multiple intents
- Requires:
  - Topic clustering
  - Intent coverage


### 8. Backlinks are declining in relative importance
- Volume less relevant
- Shift toward:
  - Mentions
  - PR
  - Brand perception


### 9. Page speed is contextual
- Critical:
  - E-commerce / transactional
- Less impactful:
  - SaaS / informational
- Exception:
  - Time to First Byte (universally important for crawling)


### 10. Organic rankings still matter
- Build trust
- Influence AI inclusion
- Support validation behavior


## Results / Outcomes

### Usability Study (AI Overviews)

- 70 participants, multiple tasks
- Key findings:
  - ~80% of final clicks go to organic results
  - Only ~1% click AI citations
  - Clicks are:
    - Fewer
    - More intentional


### Traffic Trends

- ChatGPT traffic:
  - ~700% YoY growth (SimilarWeb reference)
- Indicates rising importance of AI-driven referrals


## Limitations / Notes

- AI overviews:
  - Still appear on a fraction of queries (but growing)
- Many trends are:
  - Early-stage
  - Subject to rapid change
- Metrics and models:
  - Not yet standardized
  - Require experimentation


### Strategic Uncertainty

- SEO is transitioning:
  - Measurement unclear
  - Funnel fragmented
- Requires:
  - Short-term experimentation cycles (4–6 weeks)
  - Flexible planning (≤6 months horizon)


### Blocking AI crawlers

- Viable for:
  - Publishers
  - Affiliate-heavy businesses
- Trade-off:
  - Lose visibility vs protect content value
